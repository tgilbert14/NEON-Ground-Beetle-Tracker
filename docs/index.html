What you can do

Pick a site on the map

The Biogeography tab is a national map of every NEON site — dot size is carabid richness. Tap a dot to load it, or pick a state & site at left and it loads automatically.

Map a species' range

Choose any species and the map redraws to show where it turns up across the country, each dot sized by local abundance — a live range map for the carabid fauna.

Community & diversity

Most-abundant species by catch-per-effort, a Hill-number diversity profile (q0/q1/q2), Hurlbert rarefaction at equal sample size, and species accumulation across bouts.

Seasonality & trends

Activity-density by month, overall or per species — plus inter-annual catch-per-effort with a fitted trend and a plain-English verdict: the insect-decline view.

Biogeography

A Bray–Curtis PCoA ordination placing similar communities together, and a Dufrêne–Legendre indicator-species table naming each site's signature beetles.

Compare two sites

Put a desert next to a forest — overlay any second site on the diversity and seasonality charts to contrast richness profiles and activity curves head-to-head.

Statistics

Every view is built on transparent, well-sourced methods:

  • Abundance is effort-normalised to catch per 100 trap-nights, which also absorbs NEON's protocol changes (4→3 traps/plot in 2018; 10→6 plots/site in 2023) so sites compare fairly.
  • Richness counts species-level records only — genus/family morphospecies (Bembidion sp., Carabidae) are excluded, keyed on NEON's expert-taxonomist rank; total abundance still counts every beetle trapped, and the Diversity tab reports how many records are set aside.
  • Diversity uses Hill numbers; rarefaction (Hurlbert 1971) compares richness at equal sample size; accumulation is averaged over random bout orders (Gotelli & Colwell 2001).
  • Trends suppress the p-value under ~5 years of record — short series report the direction, not a false-precision decimal.
  • Indicator species use the Dufrêne–Legendre IndVal (specificity × fidelity), so a beetle is flagged as a site's signature only when it is both concentrated and reliably found there.
